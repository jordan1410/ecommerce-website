Mod Explanations

  • PHOB Disclaimer - PHOBs are experimental custom motherboards for the GameCube Controller that are still being iterated upon. Should you choose to purchase a PHOB, please understand that there may be unforeseen issues beyond my control as a result of their experimental status. That being said, I personally use a PHOB and believe them to be the best option for those trying to find a "forever" controller that just works with minimal maintenance required.
  • PHOB Motherboard - Custom motherboard that swaps out the standard physical stick potentiometers (see below) with magnetic components. Standard stick potentiometers have the shortest part life in the controller and impact performance the most as they degrade. This motherboard translates your inputs using magnetic fields, which will not wear down or need replacing like a physical component. Recommended for melee and ultimate.
  • Stick Potentiometers - Part inside the controller that reads inputs from the stick, having these combined with a snapback module (see below) allows your controller to function correctly. PHOB's have built-in snapback adjusting and no need for a physical snapback module.
  • Snapback Module - Eliminates snapback and calibrates your controller for things such as easier ledgedash and pivots. The module is adjustable in case snapback were to ever return to your controller due to a phenomenon known as PODE (see below).
  • PODE - Potentiometer Oddity Degradation Effect is a phenomenon that can affect your stick input readings as your stick potentiometers degrade. They degrade over time as they are used, and top players can burn through potentiometers in under 2 months with enough high-level 10-hour sessions. For most though, it is a gradual process only requiring the stick potentiometers to be replaced 1-2 times a year or when issues arise. Completely depends on how much you play and how much you are moving the stick.
  • T3 - Refers to the newest version of GameCube controller parts. Highly recommended to use T3 parts, especially stickboxes because they wear down much slower than T1/T2. T3 controllers were manufactured starting in 2004 but not all controllers since then are T3. You can identify what type your controller is by looking for a very obvious dot on the bottom left circle of your back shell. The dot will appear under and to the right of the words.
  • Trigger Potentiometers - Part inside the controller that reads the analog values when you push down the triggers. In melee, the analog values are what determine the size of your lightshield. In ultimate, once passing a certain threshold it activates the trigger press.
  • Lubed Stickboxes - Reduces the amount of wear and tear the stickbox endures from parts grinding against each other. Your stickbox will loosen at a much slower rate.
  • Lubed Triggers - Removes the violent vibration that unlubed springs have when pressed down fully.
  • Mouse Click Buttons ABXY - Places mouse click switches under the buttons. The switches are clicky and light. Recommended for people who want a more responsive and faster press.
  • Mouse Click Triggers - Places mouse click switches on the trigger pads. The switches are clicky and light. Trigger plug required to function correctly, short or long. Good overall but best for instant hard, digital presses.
  • Bald Buttons - Buttons are sanded down thus feeling ultra-smooth and easier to traverse due to their slightly reduced height and reduced friction.
  • Tactile Z Button - Makes the z button feel solid and not mushy like the traditional z. It also makes a nice clicking sound different than the mouse click sound. Recommended for people who want a more responsive press.
  • Mouse Click Z Button - Uses a mouse click switch to replace the regular z switch. A clicky and light press. Recommended for people who want a more responsive and faster press.
  • Stabilized Buttons/Triggers - Reduce wobble and remove the sound of pieces grating against the shell. Much smoother and more stable press.
  • Long Trigger Plug - Instantly get the digital press on the trigger. Meaning, there is no distance required to get to the airdodge/hard shield press for melee. You will not be able to lightshield. Recommended for someone who wants a single trigger to just powershield faster or wavedash faster etc.
  • Short Trigger Plug - Reduce the distance to the full digital press by around 50%. You can still lightshield but have access to a quicker full shield press. Recommended for someone who uses a single trigger for everything.
  • Shortened Spring - Reduce the force required to press down the trigger.
  • Removed Spring - Functions exactly the same as the long trigger plug but requires the user to hold down the trigger every time they plug in the controller. Feels worse overall.
  • Digital Triggers - Ultimate-specific mod. Makes the triggers function as buttons. There is no distance needed in the press, shield will come out immediately. However, digital triggers remove the ability to hard shield/airdodge in melee, so if you play both games, you'll have to pay extra to retain the hard shield press in melee. Retaining the hard press in melee with this mod removes the ability to lightshield. PHOB motherboards provide the function to change how the trigger functions. You can choose from a variety of settings without having to open the controller.
  • Notches - Allows you to hit those max distance wavedashes, drastic firefox angles, and never miss a shield drop.
  • Paracord - Cord looks nice!
  • Stickboxes - The higher the tier the tighter the stick. Factory New tier stick boxes are considered "tight" sticks with little to no wobble, The jump from Fac New to A is much greater than the jump from A to B. Fac News are 15-30% tighter than A. A is 5-15% tighter than Bs. Stickbox tightness is a preference thing. None of the stickboxes I sell are too loose to cause misinputs. Tighter stickboxes are not inherently better but are often preferred especially by top players.
  • Z Jump Remap Toggleable - Swaps either x and y with z providing the ability to jump with z and grab with either x or y. This gives people access to a healthier grip rather than having to claw a grip. The module can be toggled on or off. If you don't like z jump you can swap it back to default. PHOB motherboards come with the ability to swap through a combination of button presses on the controller.
  • Stick Caps - This refers to the rubber capping you touch with your fingers. Pictured qualities are shown above.
